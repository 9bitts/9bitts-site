About

Music, attention
and depth.

9bitts began as a curation of electronic music. It grew into a small room where sound, image, and thought meet — made by one person, in Leipzig.

01 — The project

Not a radio. Not a playlist.

9bitts is a curatorial project about electronic music — and what it carries when it stops being just entertainment. Each set is chosen with intention: for the energy it has, the moment it evokes, the way its tracks speak to each other.

Notes keep the room alive week to week. The journal goes deeper. The world guide maps the culture. It is a perspective — on sound, on attention, on what is worth staying for.

02 — The person

One person, one city, one project.

9bitts is a one-person project, built in Leipzig. Not a label. Not an agency. Someone who believes electronic music deserves to be taken seriously — as art, as culture, as a form of attention to the world.

Leipzig has always made space for subculture and for music that asks something of the people who listen. That is the soil.

03 — The craft

Automation in service of attention.

A custom pipeline helps publish across platforms — not to replace curation, but to free it. The real work stays human: which track, which idea, which image is worth your time.

The machine handles the rest.

The same craft that keeps this room running also builds custom web systems — platforms that need to work with real data, real users, and real care.

04 — The name

A small signal that carries something large.

The name has to do with frequency, with bits of information, with the idea that a small signal can carry something large. Electronic music works the same way — repetition with variation, structure with freedom.

Nine bits. Two t's. A small asymmetry. That is enough.

05 — How it stays alive

If this music means something to you.

There is no sponsor behind the curtain. The room stays open because listeners stay — and because some of them choose to support the work or take an object from the studio shelf.

Keep the frequency alive

Support is how one person keeps publishing sets, notes, and essays without turning the project into noise.
