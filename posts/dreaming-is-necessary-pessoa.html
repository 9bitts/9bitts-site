Fernando Pessoa · Sonhar e necessario porque sem isso a vida e pouco

Dreaming Is Not Escape. It Is the Work.

Fernando Pessoa understood something most people resist: dreaming is not laziness. It is the minimum required to make a life feel like one.

Most people treat dreaming as a luxury, something earned after the real work is done. But Fernando Pessoa seemed to believe the opposite: that without it, the rest collapses into mere routine. Not poetry as decoration. Dreaming as the thing that gives ordinary hours their weight.

Pessoa lived in Lisbon, worked a clerical job, and almost never left the city. His life, by most external measures, was narrow. But he invented entire people, each with their own biography, handwriting, and worldview. He called them heteronyms. What that tells us is that the interior can be vast even when the exterior stays still.

There is a Portuguese idea called saudade, a longing for something that may never have existed. Pessoa lived inside that feeling and made it productive. He did not try to resolve the ache. He used it. That is a different relationship to incompleteness than what most self-help culture offers, which is to fix it and move on.

The phrase sonhar é necessário, porque sem isso a vida é pouco, dreaming is necessary because without it life is too little, carries something blunt in it. Not inspirational. Almost diagnostic. Life without imagination is not tragic. It is just insufficient. That distinction matters. It asks something of you without promising anything back.

Pessoa died leaving a trunk full of unpublished manuscripts. He spent his life writing toward something he never fully named. That might sound sad. But maybe the point was never to arrive. Maybe the dreaming was the life itself, not the preparation for it. Some people only understand that too late.
